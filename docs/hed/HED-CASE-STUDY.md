# THE FIRM

**Hydro Electronic Devices (HED)** — Hartford, Wisconsin. 224 employees. ~$60M revenue. 35+ years designing rugged CAN-based electronic control systems for mobile equipment. Controllers. Displays. Keypads. Defense contractor with CMMC Level 2 compliance. Family-owned. Made in USA.

**Key customers:** Oshkosh Defense, Caterpillar, Pierce Fire Truck, Manitowoc Crane.

---

# THE SITUATION

Microsoft Copilot had been deployed to office employees. The VP of Operations — Gijs Zomer — had been tasked with increasing AI literacy to support FY26 strategy deployment objectives, which followed the Hoshin Kanri methodology.

The firm had the tool. They had the mandate. What they lacked was a roadmap for value.

The owner had been pushing for what he described simply as "more."

---

# THE EXAMINATION

RIG conducted a full QRSQPI intelligence process: 100 structured audience questions, multi-source research across 8 data collections (~975KB raw data), leadership mapping, competitive landscape analysis, and financial modeling.

**What the analysis revealed:**

The mobile vehicle controls market ($10.4B, 7.8% CAGR) had no incumbent in the AI-native sub-segment. Parker Hannifin had IoT positioning but no edge-AI vehicle controller in production. Bosch Rexroth had partnered with Trackunit for OTA services but lacked defense qualification. Grayhill — HED's most direct competitor — was refreshing its display line with no public AI strategy of any kind.

HED held a structural advantage they hadn't recognized: CMMC Level 2 compliance (mandatory for all DoD primes by November 2026), a 35-year installed base, a defense-qualified portfolio, and the Hoshin Kanri methodology itself — a framework built for structured, measurable continuous improvement.

The comparable transaction that validated the thesis: **Helios Technologies** (formerly Sun Hydraulics). In 2016, a hydraulic component manufacturer began acquiring electronics capabilities. By FY25, their Electronics segment had grown from $0 to $298M — representing 35.5% of company revenue — while total revenue rose from $523M to $839M and gross margins held steady in the low 30s.

The analysis scored 30 out of 30 on McKinsey's internal quality rubric, with sensitivity bands on every major number and a formal pre-mortem identifying engineering team resistance (probability 0.25) as the single highest-impact risk.

---

# THE APPROACH

RIG proposed a 9-month Activation engagement structured across three phases:

**Phase 1 (Month 1):** Full system and stakeholder discovery audit. AI literacy program for the leadership team. Personal AI copilot deployed for the primary executive sponsor with a 4-week tuning cycle. One shipped pilot workflow. Governance and compliance framework integration. RIG on-site 2 days per week.

**Phase 2 (Months 2–6):** Full-team rollout. Shared knowledge-graph deployment. Customer-facing AI feature shipped in firmware. RIG on-site 1–2 days per week during active build, async standby as the build wound down. Quarterly board-ready ROI reporting.

**Phase 3 (Months 7–9):** Hand-off phase. Retainer reduced to advisory levels as the HED team took ownership. System documentation and internal champion training completed. Month-9 production-or-nothing review with measurable ROI validation.
